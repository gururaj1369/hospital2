About Us



Dr. Katya pursued her MBBS from SVS Medical College. In 2011 she completed her Masters in surgery from NTR Health University with Specialization in Gynec and Obstetrics.  Later she took training from Galaxy Care Laparoscopy Institute Pune, in Gynec Laparoscopy and Robotic Surgery. As a part of Specialization she did a Fellowship in Fertility Medicine from Pune. She also received a Clinical Fellowship Certificate from Centre for Human Reproduction, Mumbai. Later, as a part of Fertility Medicine she participated in numerous National and International Conferences. In 2013, she completed a Certificate Course in Hands on Training in Assisted Re- Productive Techniques from Fertility Associates, Mumbai. In 2014, she participated in an International Conference on Standardizing Semen Analysis – 2010 Criteria at Hyderabad conducted by Academy of Clinical Embryologist. Later in 2015, she completed a Post Graduate Program in Re- Productive Endocrinology and Infertility under the guidance of The American College of Obstetrician and Gynaecologyst.

 

Best scientific practice

Kathya Testtube Baby centre  commitment to best scientific practice is supported by the quality of our in-house technical expertise. Our brand-new, state-of-the-art laboratory is led by our specially designated team, and incorporates the very latest scientific tools and techniques. For example, our semen analysis facility meets the World Health Organisation’s 6th edition standards, reflecting the latest recommended criteria for testing. 

 

Genuine care and support

Across every aspect of our practice, you’ll experience a fertility service designed around your needs, where the sharing of expert knowledge is considered just as important as your convenience and comfort.

An individual or couple’s fertility is incredibly nuanced – there is no one-size-fits-all approach. At Kathya Testtube Baby centre , we work hard to understand the underlying cause of your fertility issues before tailoring your care accordingly. In some cases, this may mean that IVF is not necessary – other treatment may suffice. In other cases, specialised IVF – involving ICSI or IMSI – may be recommended to enhance your chances of conceiving.
